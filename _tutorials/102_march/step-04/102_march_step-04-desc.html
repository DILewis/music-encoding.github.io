Step four: encoding of the first note of this example.

The <note> element in MEI describes a single pitched event. You can define single note characteristics by using the following attributes:

  • @pname (pitch name) – “c”, “d”, “e”, “f”, “g”, “a” or “b”</li>
  • @oct (octave) – pitch’s octave value, C4 being the pitch specified by the C clef “0”-”9”</li>
  • @dur (duration) – e.g. “1” for whole note, “2” for half note, “4” for quarter note, “8” for eighth note, “16” for sixteenth note, … “2048” for 2048th note

Here is an example of how to encode a whole note C4: <note pname="c" oct="4" dur="1"></note>. In MEI, notes are allowed to be children of <layer> elements.
